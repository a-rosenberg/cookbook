tempeh and broccoli rice bowl

ingredients

  • 16 oz tempeh; broken up into small bits
  • 2 Tbsp neutral oil
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 Tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1–2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 1–2 Tbsp chili sauce
  • 3 Japanese cups rice; cooked in a rice cooker
  • 1 pound broccoli florets; par-cooked
  • 2 cloves garlic; minced
  • sesame oil, to taste
  • salt, to taste
  • sesame seeds (optional)

instructions

  1. Start the rice if not already precooked.
  2. Par-cook the broccoli, then spread it out on a baking pan to dry.
  3. Preheat the broiler on high. Toss the broccoli with sesame oil and a generous pinch of salt.
  4. Broil the broccoli, tossing every few minutes, until edges are browned and crispy. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  5. Meanwhile, heat the neutral oil in a medium to medium-high skillet and brown the tempeh bits thoroughly.
  6. Once the tempeh is nicely browned, add the soy sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar, and chili sauce. Cook down until the mixture is dark and sticky.
  7. To assemble, divide the rice among bowls, top with broiled broccoli and sticky tempeh, and sprinkle with sesame seeds if using.

notes

Also great served with rice-vinegar pickles or a quick seasoned cucumber salad.
