red lentil coconut curry

ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 onion
  • 2 cloves garlic; crushed or minced
  • 2” knob of ginger; minced
  • 1 cup split red lentils (masoor dal); well rinsed
  • 1 14oz can crushed tomato or similar
  • 1 cup cilantro stems
  • 1/4 cup cilantro leaves
  • 1 14 oz can coconut milk; full fat
  • 2.5 cups water
  • (optional) 2-3 oz baby spinach
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 pinch red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 lemon
  • (optional) 1 side of steamed basmati rice

instructions

  1. Preheat saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and fry for 8 minutes, sitting frequently, until soft and caramelized. Add curry powder, pepper flakes, garlic and ginger and fry for 2 more minutes, stirring continuously.
  2. Add lentils and stir through for 1 minutes, then add tomatoes, cilantro stems, water, 1 tsp salt and a big grind of pepper.
  3. Add the coconut milk, reserving a few Tbsp if desired for finishing. Bring to a boil then simmer for 25–30 minutes, until the lentils are soft but still holding a little shape. Fold in spinach and take off heat once fully wilted. Season with salt, sugar as needed and lemon juice.
  4. Top with fresh cilantro and optional reserved coconut milk.

notes

- Probably good to double - Could probably use a whole bin of spinach. - By far, my favorite canned tomatoes are Bianco Dinapoli
